# Airbnb-Performance-Dashboard

Developed an Airbnb performance dashboard to analyze city-level trends in listings, reviews, and pricing. The analysis revealed that business is highly concentrated in a few key cities, with top locations contributing a significant share of total listings and reviews. User behavior analysis showed that the majority of reviewers engage only once or twice, indicating low repeat activity. Additionally, variations in pricing and listing distribution highlighted differences in market dynamics across cities. The project provides actionable insights into demand patterns, user engagement, and market concentration to support strategic decision-making

🎯 Business Problem
Identify which cities drive most of the business
Understand user engagement and review behavior
Analyze whether business is concentrated or evenly distributed
Evaluate demand patterns across cities
Support data-driven decision making for growth and expansion

🛠️ Tech Stack
Power BI → Used for building interactive dashboards and visualizations
DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) → Used to create measures like KPIs, cumulative %, ranking, and calculated insights

📊 Key Insights
Top cities like Paris, New York, and Sydney contribute ~50% of total listings
These same cities account for ~48% of total reviews, showing high demand concentration
Around 90%+ of users give only 1–2 reviews, indicating low repeat engagement
Business is heavily concentrated in a few cities, creating dependency risk
Some cities have high listings but not necessarily high engagement, showing quality differences
Pricing alone does not determine performance — demand (reviews) + listings together drive results

🔍 Findings / Analysis
A small number of cities dominate Airbnb’s overall performance
User activity is skewed towards one-time or low-frequency users
High-performing cities combine strong listing presence + high user demand
Market dynamics vary significantly across cities, indicating different growth opportunities
There is potential to improve engagement and expand in underperforming cities

💡 Conclusion
Airbnb’s business is not evenly distributed — it depends heavily on key cities
Improving user retention and engagement is an opportunity area
Expansion strategies should focus on replicating success of top cities
